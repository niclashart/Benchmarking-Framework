\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

We organize the analysis around six research questions. All metric values are means over 50 SQuAD questions per configuration; per-question bootstrap analyses and paired significance tests are reported in Section~\ref{subsec:stats}.

\subsection{RQ3: Prompt Template Effects}

The faithfulness gain comes at a cost: METEOR drops by 10--22pp with Detailed for models where the template takes effect. This inversion is expected---Detailed produces thorough answers with supporting evidence, aligning with retrieved context (higher faithfulness) but diverging from the brief SQuAD references (lower METEOR). BERTScore F1 is less affected (1--3pp), confirming semantic stability. This lexical-vs-semantic divergence highlights the importance of multi-metric evaluation: optimizing for METEOR alone would misleadingly favor concise, under-specified answers.

\subsection{RQ4: Chunk Size and Overlap}

We test four chunk size/overlap combinations---(500, 100), (500, 200), (1000, 100), (1000, 200)---on Qwen3-32B with recursive chunking and similarity retrieval (means over both templates). Effects are modest: faithfulness spans 0.86--0.91 and nDCG@5 spans 0.68--0.73. Smaller chunks (500) produce slightly higher nDCG@5 (+5pp) via finer granularity; larger chunks (1000) marginally raise faithfulness via broader grounding context. Semantic chunking yields identical results across all size/overlap combinations, as expected: boundaries follow embedding-similarity breakpoints, not character counts.

\subsection{RQ5: Interaction Effects}

The most striking finding is the interaction between prompt template and model size. Figure~\ref{fig:interaction} visualizes this interaction for faithfulness.

\caption{Interaction between prompt template and generation model on mean faithfulness. Larger models (Qwen3.5-397B, Qwen3.5-think, GPT-4o-mini) show gains of 9--11pp with the Detailed template, while GPT-OSS-20B shows no effect.}
\label{fig:interaction}

For GPT-OSS-20B, the Detailed template has negligible effect on faithfulness (+0.0pp). For larger models (Qwen3.5-397B, Qwen3.5-think), Detailed adds 7--9pp. This suggests that larger models better follow complex instructions to ground their answers in context, while smaller models default to parametric knowledge regardless of prompt wording.

The best configuration achieves 0.955 faithfulness: Qwen3.5-397B with recursive chunking (500/100), similarity retrieval, Detailed (configuration \#42). GPT-4o-mini reaches 0.951 with (1000/100) and similarity retrieval (\#178). The best nDCG@5 (0.825): Qwen3.5-397B with (500/200), MMR, Detailed (\#46).

The faithfulness ranking is robust: 95\% CIs are tight ($\pm 0.01$) and the top four models overlap, so sub-0.01 differences among them are not meaningful.
